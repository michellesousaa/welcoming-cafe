Our Story

From a small dream to your favorite neighborhood spot

How It All Began

In 2015, Maya and Tom Chen took a leap of faith. After years of working in corporate jobs, they decided to pursue their shared passion: creating a space where people could slow down, connect, and enjoy exceptional coffee.

They found a neglected corner storefront on Oak Street that most people walked past without a second glance. But Maya saw its potential. With exposed brick walls, original hardwood floors, and large south-facing windows, it was perfect.

After six months of renovations, much of it done by hand with help from friends and family, The Welcoming Cafe opened its doors on a rainy November morning. That first day, they served 47 customers. By the end of the year, they had become a neighborhood institution.

Our Coffee Philosophy

We believe that great coffee tells a story. That's why we partner exclusively with Stumptown Coffee Roasters, sourcing beans from family farms in Ethiopia, Colombia, Guatemala, and beyond.

Our baristas undergo rigorous training, learning not just technique but the science and art behind every extraction. Whether you're ordering a simple drip coffee or an elaborate latte, you'll taste the difference that care and expertise make.

We also believe in transparency. Ask any of our team members about where your coffee comes from, how it was processed, or why we chose a particular roast profile. We love talking coffee.

What We Stand For

Our values guide every decision we make, from the beans we source to how we treat our team.

Sustainability

We use compostable cups and packaging, source from sustainable farms, and donate our coffee grounds to local community gardens.

Community

We host weekly open mic nights, provide meeting space for local groups, and donate 2% of profits to Portland nonprofits.

Quality

We never cut corners. From our beans to our pastries to our service, we're committed to excellence in everything we do.

Inclusivity

Everyone is welcome here. We're a safe space for all people, and we celebrate the diversity of our neighborhood.
